Back to the Future

Back to the Future is a 1985 American science fiction film directed by Robert Zemeckis. It stars Michael J. Fox as Marty McFly, a teenager who is accidentally sent back in time to 1955 in a time-traveling DeLorean invented by his close friend, eccentric scientist Doc Brown (Christopher Lloyd).

The film was produced by Amblin Entertainment and distributed by Universal Pictures. It was a critical and commercial success, becoming the highest-grossing film of 1985 and receiving several Academy Award nominations, including Best Original Screenplay.
